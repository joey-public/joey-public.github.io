Problems:

Problem 1.14: A designer has available voltage amplifiers having an input resistance of 10 Kohm, an output resistance of 1 Kohm, and an open circuit voltage gain 10 V/V. The signal source has a 10 Kohm resistance and provides 10 mVrms signal, and it is required to provide a signal of at least 2 Vrms to a 1 kohm load.

a.) How many amplifier stages are required?

b.) What output voltage is actually obtained?

Solutions:

Problem 1.14: For a voltage amplifier the loaded voltage gain is:

$$ A_v = \frac{v_i}{v_o} = A_{ov} \frac{R_L}{R_L+R0} $$

The voltage into an amplifier when connected to a source with resistance $R_s$ is:

$$ v_i = v_s \frac{R_i}{R_i+R_s} $$

The problem statement provides the following parameters:

  • $v_s = 10 mV_{rms}$
  • $R_s = 10 k\Omega$
  • $A_ov = 10$
  • $R_i = 10 k\Omega$
  • $R_0 = 1 k\Omega$
  • $R_L = 1 k\Omega$

The design requirement states that cascaded amplifiers must supply $2 V_{rms}$ across the load resistance.

At the input of the first amplifier the voltage $v_i$ is:

$$ v_i = v_s \frac{R_i}{R_i+R_s} $$

$$ v_i = 10 \frac{10}{10+10} mV_{rms} $$

$$ v_i = 5 mV_{rms} $$

So in order to achieve the $2 V_{rms}$ requirement the total gain must be:

$$ A_{target} = \frac{2000}{5} = 400 $$

The voltage gain of one amplifier loaded with another amplifier is:

$$ A_{v1} = A_v \frac{R_i}{R_i+R_o} $$

$$ A_{v1} = 10 \frac{10}{11} $$

$$ A_{v1} = 9.09 $$

The voltage gain of an amplifier loaded with the given load resistance is:

$$ A_{v1} = A_v \frac{R_L}{R_L+R_o} $$

$$ A_{v1} = 10 \frac{1}{2} $$

$$ A_{v1} = 5 $$

To find the number of amplifiers needed solve the following equation:

$$ A_{target} = A_{v1}^n * A_{v2} $$

$$ 400 = 9.09^n * 5 $$

Which gives $n=2$ so that $\boxed{n+1=3}$ amplifiers are needed to achieve the design requirements.

With 3 amplifiers the actual output voltage is:

$$ v_o = A_{v_1}^2 * A_{v2} * v_i $$

$$ v_o = 9.09^2 * 5 * 5 mV_{rms} $$

$$ \boxed{ v_o = 2.07 V} $$

Problem 1.16: The problem statement provides:

  • $R_s = 2 k\Omega$
  • $R_i = 2 k\Omega$
  • $R_o = 20 k\Omega$
  • $G_m = 40 ms$
  • $R_L = 1 k\Omega$

To solve for the total voltage gain from $v_i$ to $v_o$:

$$ v_o = G_m v_i \left[\frac{1}{R_o}+\frac{1}{R_L}\right]^{-1} $$

$$ A_{v1} = \frac{v_o}{v_i} = G_m \left[\frac{1}{R_o}+\frac{1}{R_L}\right]^{-1} $$

$$ A_{v1} = \frac{v_o}{v_i} = 0.040 \left[\frac{1}{20000}+\frac{1}{1000}\right]^{-1} $$

$$ \boxed{ A_{v1} = 38.1 V/V } $$

To find the voltage gain from the source to the load:

$$ A_{v2} = \frac{v_o}{v_s} $$

where:

$$ v_i = v_s \frac{R_i}{R_i + R_s} $$

$$ v_s = 2 v_i $$

$$ A_{v2} = \frac{v_o}{2 v_i} = 0.5 A_{v1} $$

$$ \boxed{ A_{v1} = 19.05 V/V } $$

1.17: Applying a text voltage $v_x$ to the amplifuers input and using KCL on the input node gives:

$$ i_x = \frac{v_x}{R_i} + g_m v_x $$

solving for the input resistance as $v_x / i_x$ gives:

$$ \frac{i_x}{v_x} = \frac{1}{R_i} + g_m $$

$$ \frac{v_x}{i_x} = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{R_i} + g_m} $$

$$ \boxed{ \frac{v_x}{i_x} = \frac{R_i}{1 + R_i g_m} } $$
