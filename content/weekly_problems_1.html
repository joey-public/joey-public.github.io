Sedra/Smith Section 1.4: Amplifiers

Problems:

Problem 1.7: An amplifier fed by a sine-wave signal of 5 mV peak delivers a sine-wave output of 1 V peak to a load resistance of 2 K-ohm. The input current of the amplifier is found to be a sine wave of 5 uA peak. Calculate the voltage gain, current gain, and power gain as ratios and in decibels.

Problem 1.9: An amplifier operating from a +/-15 V power supply delivers an output sine-wave signal of 20V peak to peak to a 2 K-ohm load resistance. The amplifier draws an average current of 1.7 mA from each of its two power supplies and a negligible current from the input signal source. Find the power dissipated in the amplifier circuit.

Problem 1.10: An amplifier operating from a +/- 15 V power supply has a linear transfer characteristic except for output saturation at +/- 13 V. If the amplifier gain is 100 V/V, find the rms value of the largest sine wave signal that can be applied at the input without clipping.
